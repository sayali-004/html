LORD SHIVA



  • Lord shiva temples

The glory of Lord Shiva is widespread as he is known to bless his devotees with a happy and blissful married life and his worshippers adore him for generous boons. Lord Shiva is the supreme power as per the religious beliefs of Hindus, who has played a vital role in Indian culture. In mythologies, he has been for his contrast personality like a peaceful entity as well as a fierce deity. In diverse states of India, Lord Shiva is being worshipped in different forms. This is the reason; there are several temples of Lord Shiva in India. The festival of Mahashivratri celebrates the wedding day of Lord Shiva with Goddess Parvathi. On this day, a grand celebration is organized and worshipping is done to the Shivlingam. People usually keep fast on this special eve. A number of Shiva temples in India are there to celebrate this special occasion on grand level.

  1. kedarnath-temple,Uttarakhand


    In Uttarakhand state of India, Kedarnath is a popular Shiva temple that is one of the key destinations of 'Chardham Yatra'. Situated on the Garhwal Himalayan Range close to Mandakini River, it is a highly respected Shiva Temple in all over of the world among the Hindus. The temple is located at an elevation of about 3583 meters and it receives great snowfall in winters. On the auspicious celebration of Mahashivratri, a grand celebration takes place.

  2. Trimbakeshwar Shiva Temple, Nasik


    3. Located between three hills named Brahmagiri, Nilagiri and Kalagiri, Trimbakeshwar Shiva Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated Lord Shiva. The temple houses three lingams representing Shiv, Vishnu and Brahma. The present day temple was constructed by Peshwa Balaji Baji Rao. It features a holy pond called Amritavarshini. There are more water bodies in this temple. The Shiva temple is visited by a number of devotees on a daily basis.


  3. Mangueshi Temple, Goa


    4. Dedicated to Lord Manguesh, an incarnation of Lord Shiva, Mangueshi Temple is one of the most visited temples of Goa. It is located at Mangeshi Village of Ponda taluk in Goa. The main deity of the temple is worshipped here as Shivlinga making it one of the most renowned temples of Lord Shiva. Visitors of the temple have to follow a dress code while visiting. The temple complex also enshrines the Goddess Parvathi and God Ganesha. There are idols of many other gods and goddesses in this temple.

  4. Amarnath Temple, Srinagar


    5. Amarnath cave is the most regarded Shiva temple of India, which is located in Jammu and Kashmir. Positioned at an altitude of 3,888 meters, the shrine plays an important role in the life of the followers of Hinduism. The cave is surrounded by snowcapped mountains and is covered with snow almost maximum part of the year except summer when it is open for pilgrims. Amarnath turns into a pilgrimage center for thousand of Hindu devotees every year who visit the place challenging mountainous terrains.

  5. Ramanathaswamy Temple, Rameswaram


    6. Located in Rameswaram Island of the Tamil Nadu state of India, Ramanathaswamy Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the lord Shiva. It is also among the 12 Jyotirlinga temples of Lord Shiva. This Shiva temple was expanded during the 12th century by Pandya Dynasty, and its main shrine sanctum was restored by Jeyaveera Cinkaiariyan and his successor Gunaveera Cinkaiariyan of the Jaffna kingdom. Having the longest corridor amidst all Hindu temples of India, it is a must visit attraction of the city.
